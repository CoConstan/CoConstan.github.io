<h2>Some videos for theoritical background :</h2>
<figure class="post__video"><iframe width="560" height="314" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/FBpPjjhJGhk" allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" data-mce-fragment="1"></iframe></figure>
<p>You can start by looking at <a href="https://youtu.be/FBpPjjhJGhk" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">But what is a neural network REALLY</a> by Algorithmic Simplicity. To go further the whole <a href="https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3b1b's Neural networks playlist</a> is a really good starting point. Once you finish that playlist you can return to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@algorithmicsimplicity" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Algorithmic Simplicity's channel</a> and look for video about specific model. </p>
<p>The first two channels focus heavily on mathematics, so if you're seeking a perspective more aligned with computer science, consider checking out Sebastien Lague's video : <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hfMk-kjRv4c">How to Create a Neural Network (and Train it to Identify Doodles)</a>. </p>
<p>Before jumping into Pytorch and Tensorflow, you can try to build your own neural network from scratch to see if you fully understand the basics of backpropagation and neural network. </p>
